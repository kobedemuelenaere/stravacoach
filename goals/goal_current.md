# Current Goal

**Primary:** Mountain hiking in the Dolomites  
**Date:** July 20, 2026  
**Duration:** Multi-day mountain hikes  
**Target:** Complete long mountain days (8+ hours) at firm pace without exhaustion; maintain effort through elevation and duration

## Secondary Targets (running fitness)

- Build a strong, high-capacity running base for long-distance coverage
- Comfortable 21 km running distance without complete fatigue
- Start **trail running** as a core training modality (technical footwork, uneven terrain, strength in varied conditions)
- Develop efficient pace on both road and trail
- Maintain tempo/interval capacity alongside endurance
- Prepare body for sustained effort + recovery on consecutive days

## What Success Looks Like

1. **On the mountain:** Handle **2000m elevation gain in a single day** at sustainable pace without exhaustion. Walk/hike strong for 6–8+ hours daily, maintain good climbing rhythm, handle long descents with strong legs (no quad failure), end day with energy and confidence reserves.
2. **In running:** Run 21 km comfortably on road; be comfortable on trail terrain (technical, varied ground); faster paces (6:00–6:20/km) feel controlled on both surfaces; HR is stable; no sharp fatigue or injury.
3. **Physically:** Strong descending power (critical for long mountain days); solid ankle/core stability on uneven terrain; trail-specific leg strength and proprioception; no pattern of injury or overuse.

## Training Architecture

**Duration:** ~12 weeks (April 29 – July 20)  
**Volume structure:** 2–3 runs per week + strength/mobility  
**Run types:**
- Easy runs (Z2, 6:10–6:50/km) — mix of road and trail
- Long runs (building to 16–18 km by July) — transition to trail as distance increases
- Tempo/fartlek/interval (one per week, built into the plan) — both road and trail variants
- **Trail-specific sessions** — technical terrain, varied elevation, strength on descents

**Strength focus:**
- Descending power (quads, glutes, eccentric load)
- Ankle stability and calf strength
- Core engagement
- Hip mobility
- 1–2x per week integrated into running schedule

**Schedule preference:** Long run on weekend; tempo/quality during the week; willing to do back-to-back training days if structured correctly

## Notes

- **Why this goal:** Mountain hiking in the Dolomites is the primary driver, but improving as a runner is an equal part of it — not just base fitness for hiking, but genuine running improvement. Kobe wants to build a "high solid base" that allows covering long distances with strength and efficiency, not just survival.
- **The 2000m target:** This is the single-day elevation benchmark for Dolomites fitness. It defines strength and endurance demands. Training will include hill repeats, sustained climbing on long runs, and eccentric leg work for descending.
- **Trail running:** Starting trail running is essential for (a) foot/ankle proprioception on real terrain, (b) quad and calf strength on varied ground, (c) technical footwork, (d) mental confidence on non-paved surfaces. Trails will be mixed into the plan from week 1, progressing in difficulty and distance.
- **The blend:** Running fitness and hiking fitness are synergistic. VO2max, leg strength, work capacity, and terrain-specific skills all transfer. Descending power and ankle stability must be deliberately trained — this is often neglected but critical for mountain days.
- **Volume progression:** Current ~20 km/week is low-to-moderate. Will build gradually toward 25–30 km/week in June–July, with a taper in the final weeks before July 20.
- **No race goal:** This is performance-oriented, not race-time focused. Success is capability and confidence on the mountain, not a clock.
