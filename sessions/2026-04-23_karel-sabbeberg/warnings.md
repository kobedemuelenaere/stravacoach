# Warnings — 6,5 × Karel Sabbeberg (April 23)

## Significant pace fade across reps — fatigue signal

**The data:** Ascent pace dropped 37% from rep 1 to rep 5 (7:28 → 10:18 /km). While some fade is normal, 37% is substantial and indicates cumulative fatigue.

**Why it matters:** This pace degradation, combined with HR climbing from 149→167 bpm, shows your legs and nervous system were fatiguing across the set. It's not a failure (the session was still completed), but it signals you're near fatigue limits.

**Going forward:** Watch for this pattern in the next hard session. If pace fade exceeds 25% and HR creeps above 170 bpm, reduce reps or intensity — it's an early warning sign of overreaching.

## HR never recovered between climbs — accumulated cardiovascular load

**The data:** HR stayed in Z3–Z4 (143–175 bpm) during all 6 descent recoveries. Never dropped to Z2 (124–143 bpm), the true parasympathetic recovery zone.

**Why it matters:** Your parasympathetic nervous system (vagal tone) drives recovery between efforts. When HR stays elevated throughout a session, your autonomic nervous system never gets the signal to reset, and systemic fatigue accumulates faster than adaptation.

**What to do:** On future hill repeats, deliberately slow descents to allow HR to drop below 143 bpm (Z2 range) for at least 30–60 seconds between climbs. This forces recovery and makes you fresher for the next climb. You'll actually complete reps stronger this way.

## Three hard sessions in 6 days — fatigue stacking pattern

**The data:**
- 4/18: 88-minute fartlek (threshold work, Z3–Z4)
- 4/20: 5 × 2 min intervals (VO2max, hard)
- 4/23: 6 × hill repeats (strength + intensity, high HR)

**The concern:** If recovery days (4/19, 4/21, 4/22) were not genuinely easy (Z1–Z2, slow pace, <60% max HR), you've accumulated significant CNS and metabolic load without adequate stimulus-to-recovery ratio. Early signs include:
- Pace fade (seen here)
- Elevated resting HR (check tomorrow morning)
- Sleep quality dips
- Persistent muscle soreness

**Action:** Confirm the next 2–3 days are easy or off. No moderate sessions, no strength work. Just easy running (6:30–7:30 /km pace, Z1–Z2 HR 110–143 bpm) or complete rest. Your body needs the adaptation window.
