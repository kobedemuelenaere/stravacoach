## Effort assessment

**Fitness-wise: excellent. Intent-wise: wrong intensity.**

You held 6:17 /km pace with zero HR drift for 76 minutes in Z3 (threshold). That demonstrates:
- Strong aerobic capacity
- Excellent pacing discipline
- Fitness to sustain hard effort over distance

But a "long run" in training means easy + distance, not hard + distance. Easy runs (Z1–Z2, 6:45–7:30 /km) build aerobic base and enable recovery. Threshold runs (Z3, 6:10–6:25 /km) build pace fitness but demand recovery afterward. A 12 km threshold run is hard work, not a recovery session.

## What you did well

1. **Pacing consistency.** 6:11 → 6:24 /km across 12 km with zero HR drift is elite-level stability. You held your rhythm perfectly.
2. **Mental toughness.** 76 minutes in Z3 is genuinely hard. You stayed composed and controlled throughout.
3. **Recovery awareness shown here:** You chose a flat, scenic route (3 kastelen) and took it at midday (good light and temperature). Smart logistics.

## What to work on

1. **Long-run intent.** Next long run: target Z1–Z2 (easy) pace. Aim for 6:45–7:30 /km, keep HR <143 bpm. Build distance at conversational pace, not threshold. Your goal is fitness and distance tolerance, not pace.
2. **Recovery sequencing.** After a hard session (hill repeats on 4/23), the next session should be genuinely easy or off — not another hard session. Even though you executed this run perfectly, back-to-back hard efforts without recovery is fatigue-stacking.

## Verdict

**Partially.** You executed perfectly (pacing, stability, toughness), but this was the wrong type of long run for this point in your training. A long run should be easy; this was hard. That matters for how your body adapts.
